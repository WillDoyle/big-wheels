Bio

Soft-rock sweethearts Big Wheels are catapulting their ethereal blend of modern-day confessional songwriting and colourful retro allure into 2023 without hesitation.
The release of their first two singles ‘Chain of Fire’ and ‘All I Ever Wanna Do’ has sent the Sydney-based four-piece onto a trail of both national and international recognition. Collectively the tracks have made airwaves on BBC Music Introducing, Triple J Unearthed, US College Radio, Double J, FBi Radio, 3RRR and Boogaloo Radio in the UK. “ a catchy as heck love song with big timeless appeal “ Declan Byrne (Triple J) review of ‘All I Ever Wanna Do’. August 2022 The undeniable songwriting connection between co-captains Cass Ford & Lawson Doyle exemplifies their combining and different perspectives on romance, ego, life and individuality, taking you down a rabbit hole of comforting relatability. Between a unique cosmic blend of classic rock & roll, blues and country, together they draw heavy inspiration from the likes of Fleetwood Mac, Oasis, Neil Young & Jefferson Airplane. Winding back to the beginnings of Big Wheels, these three effervescent wildcards entered into each other's bubbles with nothing less than perfect timing. Having crossed paths fatefully back in 2016, the fire was lit out of Doyle and Ford’s mutual love and respect for one another’s vivacity and style. Doyle had spent the past 5 years of his career performing across festival stages and selling out rooms with two respective outfits Sunflower & Port Royal while Ford, no stranger to a life on the road, had spent her childhood years touring Australia with a mother and father both in the music biz. Drummer Dale Coombes made his timely entrance via a mutual friend after years of holding only a degree of separation from the pair. “ Singer Cass Ford has the kind of velvet vocals that entice and mesmerise - a touch of Stevie Nicks - that are embellished by the delicate harmonies “ Arun Kendall (Backseat Mafia) on ‘Chain of Fire’. May 2022 In a whirlwind of uncertain times late 2021, the trio leapt into their early creative experimentation, while taking up residence in a sleepy suburb on the Sunshine Coast, QLD. It was here, they came to the conclusion that a move to the big smoke of Sydney was inevitably on the horizon. Since then, the band has landed support slots with heavy-hitters San Cisco, Pacific Avenue, Jack Botts, DICE and Rum Jungle along with a string of successful festival appearances up and down the east coast. In September 2022, Big Wheels debuted a live studio performance of Chain of Fire on ABC TV’s latest talk show Frankly with Fran Kelly. The brand continues on a steady trajectory which has so far lead to a number of notable collaborations including a recent clothing sponsorship from Wrangler Australia. With their newest soft-rock swinger ‘Colourblind’ due early February 2023, Big Wheels are gearing up for a huge year of new milestones as they release their ever-growing eclectic and vibrant catalogue.
